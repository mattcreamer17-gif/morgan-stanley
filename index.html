The queue behind the flagship three

The AI @ Morgan Stanley Assistant is live across 98 percent of advisor teams. Debrief has moved from pilot to full deployment across the 15,000-advisor population. AskResearchGPT is in production in Global Research. Those were the clean problems. The queue behind them is harder, not because the models are weaker, but because the governance gate now has to separate genuine AI problems from rule-based compliance logic, from workflow orchestration, from data-platform work that should sit with a different team entirely.

A frame for the next wave

Most firms that get to the scale Morgan Stanley has reached on Assistant and Debrief discover that roughly 60 percent of the next wave of proposed use cases are traditional code and database problems, 30 percent are rule-based logic with a narrow AI extraction step, and only 10 percent are genuine model problems. Putting an LLM on the first 60 percent is how eval gates fail, how compliance reviews stretch into quarters, and how the advisor productivity gains from Debrief get burned back up in integration overhead. The question that matters for the next year is which layer each piece of a problem belongs on, and how to version that decision so the firmwide AI steering group can audit it later.

A practical reference point

Correlation One ran workforce AI enablement across Pacific Life and Colgate-Palmolive starting May 2025. 1,500+ people trained. 6,000 to 9,000 hours per year saved, measured. 95 percent of trainees still using the tools 30 days after the workshop. The design of that engagement was not a generic AI course. It was a workflow-capture exercise that produced a prioritized orchestration-layer spec before the first session was delivered. The same shape applies to Morgan Stanley's next wave of advisor enablement, particularly in the post-Debrief handoff into CRM, compliance logging, and financial planning.

A second reference, closer to leadership-level enablement: KPMG UK, one of the Big Four. 40+ executives trained, regulated-industry audience, cohort format.

The methodology is published

Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM TiiS. Layered context from L0 identity through L4 working artifacts. Measurable interpretability and reproducibility. Repo: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.

The Ethics Engine, a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs, sits alongside as a governance-adjacent instrument. Paper: arxiv.org/abs/2510.11742. Repo: github.com/RinDig/AuditEngine.
